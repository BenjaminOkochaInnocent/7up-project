About 7UP

7 Up was created by Charles Leiper Grigg, in 1920. Grigg came up with the formula for a lemon-lime soft drink in 1929. The product, originally named "Bib-Label Lithiated Lemon-Lime Soda". It contained lithium citrate, a mood-stabilizing drug, until 1948. It was one of a number of patent medicine products popular in the late-19th and early-20th centuries. Its name was later shortened to "7 Up Lithiated Lemon Soda" before being further shortened to just "7 Up" by 1936.

Enjoy the deliciously uncomplicated taste of Simple 7UP, a soda made with only real ingredients. How refreshing.
